#### 1.1.3 Key Genus-Specific IAWA Feature Profiles

**Dalbergia (Rosewoods, Fabaceae) — CITES Appendix II (except D. nigra = I):**
Diffuse-porous. Vessels solitary + radial multiples 2-3, narrow (≤200µm). Simple perforations. Vasicentric + aliform + confluent parenchyma. Rays 1-4 seriate, STORIED. Vessel elements, parenchyma, and fibers also storied — full storied structure. Dark gum deposits in heartwood vessels common. Heartwood dark purple-brown to black with irregular darker streaks. Very dense (800-1200 kg/m³).

**Swietenia (Mahoganies, Meliaceae) — S. macrophylla CITES II (Neotropical only):**
Diffuse-porous. Vessels solitary + radial multiples 2-3, medium (100-200µm). Simple perforations. Reddish-brown gum deposits. Vasicentric + confluent + marginal parenchyma. Rays 1-5 seriate, storied. Growth rings distinct to indistinct (tropical). Medium density (520-590 kg/m³). Heartwood reddish-brown, darkens with age.

**Quercus (Oaks, Fagaceae):**
Ring-porous (white + red oaks). Earlywood vessels very large (200-400µm) in continuous row. Latewood vessels small, in flame-like/dendritic arrangement. Simple perforations. Tyloses: ABUNDANT in white oak (Q. alba group), sparse/absent in red oak (Q. rubra group). Parenchyma: apotracheal diffuse-in-aggregates + paratracheal scanty. Rays: TWO DISTINCT SIZES — very broad (>1mm, multiseriate up to 30+ cells) + fine (uniseriate). Medium to high density (630-770 kg/m³).

**Tectona grandis (Teak, Lamiaceae):**
Ring-porous to semi-ring-porous. Earlywood vessels solitary, large, may contain white deposits. Latewood vessels smaller, solitary. Simple perforations. Vasicentric + marginal parenchyma. Rays 1-4 seriate, non-storied. Growth rings distinct (tropical). Golden-brown heartwood. Contains tectoquinone (oily feel). Density 520-690 kg/m³.

**Shorea (Meranti/Balau groups, Dipterocarpaceae):**
Diffuse-porous. Vessels solitary + radial multiples. Simple perforations. Paratracheal + apotracheal banded parenchyma. Rays 1-5 seriate. DIAGNOSTIC: Axial intercellular canals in tangential lines (present in all Dipterocarpaceae). Density ranges by group: Light Red Meranti 370-570, Dark Red Meranti 580-680, Yellow Balau 880-970 kg/m³.

**Pinus (Pines, Pinaceae):**
SOFTWOOD. Growth rings distinct. Transition: abrupt (hard pines, subg. Pinus) or gradual (soft pines, subg. Strobus). Axial resin canals PRESENT with THIN-walled epithelial cells (Pinus-type, diagnostic). Ray tracheids present. Cross-field pits: fenestriform (soft pines) or pinoid (hard pines). Density varies: Eastern White Pine 380-420, Scots Pine 480-530, Southern Yellow Pine 550-690 kg/m³.

**Entandrophragma (Sapele, Meliaceae):**
Diffuse-porous. Vessels solitary + radial multiples 2-4. Simple perforations. Narrow marginal parenchyma bands + narrow regular bands (diagnostic for Meliaceae). Rays 1-5 seriate, STORIED. Growth rings distinct. Medium density (590-650 kg/m³). Heartwood dark reddish-brown with interlocked grain producing ribbon stripe figure.

**Khaya (African Mahogany, Meliaceae):**
Diffuse-porous. Vessels solitary + radial multiples. Simple perforations. Vasicentric + narrow regular bands of parenchyma. Rays 1-4 seriate, STORIED. Growth rings often indistinct. Medium density (490-560 kg/m³). Heartwood pinkish-brown.

**Millettia laurentii (Wenge, Fabaceae):**
Diffuse-porous. Vessels solitary + radial multiples 2-3. Simple perforations. Vasicentric + aliform + confluent parenchyma. Rays 1-3 seriate, STORIED. Growth rings distinct. Heartwood dark brown with black streaks. Very coarse texture. Density 810-890 kg/m³.

**Diospyros (Ebony, Ebenaceae):**
Diffuse-porous. Vessels solitary + radial multiples 2-3, very small (≤100µm), numerous (>40/mm²). Simple perforations. Vasicentric parenchyma. Rays 1-2 seriate (exclusively uniseriate in some species), STORIED (low rays). Growth rings indistinct. Heartwood: jet black in core species (D. crassiflora, D. celebica), streaked in others. Extremely dense (900-1150 kg/m³).

**Intsia bijuga (Merbau, Fabaceae):**
Diffuse-porous. Vessels solitary + radial multiples 2-3. Simple perforations. Vasicentric + aliform + confluent parenchyma. Rays 1-4 seriate, STORIED. Distinctive sulfur-yellow powder in vessels (soluble extractives). Heartwood yellowish to orange-brown. Density 760-850 kg/m³.

**Acacia (Koa, Fabaceae — and other commercial Acacia):**
Diffuse-porous. Vessels solitary + radial multiples. Simple perforations. Vasicentric + confluent + marginal parenchyma. Rays 1-3 seriate. Variable storied structure by species. Growth rings distinct to indistinct. Density highly variable: A. koa 560-630, A. melanoxylon (Australian Blackwood) 640-750 kg/m³.

**Betula (Birch, Betulaceae):**
Diffuse-porous. Vessels solitary + radial multiples 2-4, small (50-100µm). SCALARIFORM perforation plates (diagnostic, 10-30 bars in B. alleghaniensis, 15-30 in B. pendula). Apotracheal diffuse-in-aggregates parenchyma + scanty paratracheal. Rays 1-4 seriate. Growth rings distinct. Medium density (610-710 kg/m³).

**Acer (Maple, Sapindaceae):**
Diffuse-porous. Vessels solitary + radial multiples 2-5, small (≤100µm). Simple perforations (most species) or scalariform (few bars, A. saccharum sometimes). Apotracheal diffuse-in-aggregates parenchyma + scanty paratracheal. Rays 1-5 seriate. Growth rings distinct. Density: Sugar Maple 690-740, Red Maple 530-610 kg/m³.

**Fraxinus (Ash, Oleaceae):**
Ring-porous. Earlywood vessels large (200-350µm), in continuous row 1-3 vessels wide. Latewood vessels small, solitary + clusters, often in short radial multiples. Simple perforations. Vasicentric + confluent parenchyma. Rays 1-4 seriate. Growth rings very distinct. Density 640-710 kg/m³.

**Juglans (Walnut, Juglandaceae):**
Semi-ring-porous to diffuse-porous. Vessels solitary + radial multiples 2-3. Simple perforations (rare scalariform in some). Parenchyma: apotracheal diffuse-in-aggregates + marginal bands. Rays 1-5 seriate. Growth rings distinct. Heartwood dark brown with darker streaks. Density 610-660 kg/m³.

**Pterocarpus (Padauk, Fabaceae):**
Diffuse-porous. Vessels solitary + radial multiples 2-3. Simple perforations. Vasicentric + aliform + confluent parenchyma (often in eye-shaped bands). Rays 1-4 seriate, STORIED. Growth rings distinct to indistinct. Heartwood vivid red to orange-red (P. soyauxii) or deep red-purple (P. santalinus). Density: P. soyauxii 720-800, P. santalinus 900-1050, P. erinaceus 650-800, P. macrocarpus 810-880 kg/m³. P. santalinus = CITES II. P. erinaceus = CITES II.

**Machaerium (Pau Ferro / Santos Rosewood, Fabaceae):**
Diffuse-porous. Very similar to Dalbergia. Vessels solitary + radial multiples. Simple perforations. Aliform + confluent parenchyma. Rays 1-3 seriate, STORIED. Growth rings distinct. Heartwood dark brown to violet-brown with darker streaks. Density 820-900 kg/m³. KEY DISTINCTION from Dalbergia: requires DART-MS or DNA — macroscopic features overlap.

**Pseudotsuga menziesii (Douglas Fir, Pinaceae):**
SOFTWOOD. Growth rings very distinct. Abrupt earlywood-latewood transition. Axial resin canals present with THICK-walled epithelial cells. Ray tracheids present. Cross-field pits: piceoid. Spiral thickening in tracheids (diagnostic). Density 470-540 kg/m³.

**Eucalyptus (Jarrah and many species, Myrtaceae):**
Diffuse-porous. Vessels solitary (often exclusively in some species), variable size. Simple perforations. Vasicentric + confluent parenchyma. Rays 1-2 seriate (often exclusively uniseriate). Growth rings distinct to indistinct. Many species have high density (790-1100 kg/m³) and high shrinkage (T/R >2.0). Some species contain kino veins (gum veins).

**Microberlinia (Zebrawood, Fabaceae):**
Diffuse-porous. Vessels solitary + radial multiples 2-3, medium-large. Simple perforations. Vasicentric + aliform-confluent parenchyma. Rays 1-4 seriate, most STORIED. Growth rings distinct. DIAGNOSTIC: Regular bands of parenchyma creating alternating dark/light zebra pattern on quarter-sawn surface. Heartwood light golden with dark brown streaks. Density 740-810 kg/m³.

**Milicia excelsa (Iroko, Moraceae):**
Diffuse-porous. Vessels solitary + radial multiples 2-3, large. Simple perforations. Vasicentric + aliform parenchyma. Rays 1-5 seriate, most STORIED. Growth rings distinct. Heartwood yellow-brown to dark brown. Contains calcium carbonate deposits visible as white specks on end-grain. Density 630-680 kg/m³.

#### 1.3.2 Mechanical Property Formulas (USDA FPL Generalized)

For hardwoods (G = specific gravity = air-dry density/1000):
- MOR (MPa) ≈ 170 × G + 60
- MOE (GPa) ≈ 23 × G + 5
- Crushing strength (MPa) ≈ 100 × G + 30
- Janka hardness (N) ≈ 14000 × G - 2000

For softwoods (G = specific gravity):
- MOR (MPa) ≈ 180 × G + 30
- MOE (GPa) ≈ 21 × G + 3
- Crushing strength (MPa) ≈ 80 × G + 20
- Janka hardness (N) ≈ 11000 × G - 1500

#### 1.4.3 CITES Annotation for Dalbergia spp. (#15)

All Dalbergia species (excluding D. nigra) listed on Appendix II with Annotation #15 effective Jan 2017:
- **Regulated:** Logs, sawn wood, veneer sheets, plywood, wood chips, unfinished wood articles.
- **Exempt:** Seeds, spores, pollen, seedling/tissue cultures, leaves, flowers, fruits.
- **Exempt:** Finished musical instruments (completed and ready to play), finished musical instrument parts, finished musical instrument accessories — each shipment ≤10kg total.
- **Exempt:** Finished wood products ≤10kg per shipment (furniture, turned items, small handicrafts).
- **Exempt:** Non-commercial personal/household effects ≤10kg.

#### 1.4.4 National and Regional Timber Legality Frameworks

**EU Timber Regulation (EUTR) / EU Deforestation Regulation (EUDR):**
- EUTR (995/2010): Due diligence system required. Bans illegally harvested timber.
- EUDR (2023/1115): Expands scope beyond timber. Effective Dec 2024/2025.
- Requires: species name (scientific), harvest country + sub-national region, geo-coordinates of harvest plots, due diligence statement, risk assessment.
- FLEGT-licensed timber from VPA countries = automatically compliant.
- Fines: proportional to environmental damage, confiscation, trading ban.

**US Lacey Act (16 U.S.C. §§ 3371-3378):**
- Requires import declaration: scientific name, harvest country, quantity, value (PPQ Form 505).
- Illegal to trade in violation of ANY foreign law (the "underlying offense" provision).
- Penalties: civil up to $10,000; criminal up to $500,000 + 5 years imprisonment; forfeiture of goods.
- 2008 amendments specifically added plant products (previously only wildlife).

**VNFOREST / VNTLAS (Vietnam):**
- Decree 102/2020/ND-CP: Vietnam Timber Legality Assurance System.
- Import requirements: valid export permit from origin + CITES permit + bill of lading + invoice + packing list.
- Domestic timber: forest owner certification + harvest permit + transport documents.
- Enterprise: maintain Timber Legality Assurance Manuals.
- Vietnam is a VPA country (EU FLEGT); FLEGT licensing operational.
- High-risk species list maintained by VNFOREST.

**Japan Clean Wood Act (2020):**
- Legality verification required for timber imports.
- Goho Wood registration system.
- Requires harvest legality documentation from country of origin.

**Australia Illegal Logging Prohibition Act (2012):**
- Prohibits import and processing of illegally logged timber.
- Due diligence requirements for importers and processors.

## 3. State-of-the-Art Methods & Tools

### 3.1 Species Identification Cascade

Most reliable identification from cheapest to most definitive:

1. **Macroscopic end-grain examination (10-40x):** IAWA feature coding for genus-level ID. Requires clean end-grain surface. Accuracy: 70-90% genus-level, <50% species-level for most commercial groups.

2. **Stereomicroscopy (30-50x):** 3D surface examination. Vessel, parenchyma, ray detail. Used for initial screening and morphotype sorting.

3. **Thin-section light microscopy (100-400x):** Transmitted light. Requires microtome sections. Reveals perforation plates, pits, crystals, ray cellular detail. Genus-level with higher confidence; some species discrimination possible.

4. **DART-MS (Direct Analysis in Real Time Mass Spectrometry):** Chemometric fingerprinting. Rapid (seconds), minimal sample prep. Excellent for Dalbergia, Swietenia, and CITES-protected genera. Requires spectral library reference. Accuracy: 85-95% genus/species for well-characterized groups.

5. **DNA barcoding (rbcL + matK + trnH-psbA + ITS2):** Species-level identification. Can distinguish closely related species. Requires: DNA extraction from wood (challenging for processed/old wood), PCR amplification, sequencing, reference database matching. Success rate highly variable with wood age and processing.

6. **Stable isotope analysis (D/H, ¹⁸O, ¹³C, ¹⁵N, ³⁴S):** Geographic origin verification. Isotope ratios reflect environmental growing conditions. Can distinguish plantations from natural forest. Can verify claimed harvest region.

7. **Machine vision / CNN-based automated ID:** Trained on InsideWood and reference collections. Macroscopic image → genus prediction. Emerging technology; accuracy improving rapidly. Commercial systems available (Xylotron, MyWood-ID).
